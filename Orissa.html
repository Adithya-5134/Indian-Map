Odisha

Konark Sun Temple - A UNESCO World Heritage Site

Quick Facts

  • Area: 155,707 Sq. Kms
  • Capital: Bhubaneswar
  • Language: Odia
  • Population: 4,19,74,218
  • Famous for: Jagannath Temple, Puri Beach, Konark Sun Temple, Chilika Lake, Ratha Yatra
  • Major Cities: Bhubaneswar, Cuttack, Rourkela

Geographical & Economic Importance

Odisha has a long coastline along the Bay of Bengal, contributing significantly to **maritime trade and fishing industries**. It is rich in **mineral resources**, including **iron ore, coal, and bauxite**, making it one of India's major industrial hubs.

Historical Significance

Odisha is historically known for the **Kalinga Empire**, which was the site of the famous **Kalinga War** fought by Emperor Ashoka. The war led to Ashoka's transformation and the spread of **Buddhism**. The **Konark Sun Temple** and **Jagannath Temple** are major heritage landmarks.

Cultural Significance

Odisha has a vibrant culture with classical dance forms like **Odissi**, which is one of the oldest dance forms in India. The **Ratha Yatra of Puri** is a world-famous festival celebrated annually. The state is also known for its exquisite **Pattachitra paintings and silver filigree work**.

Tourist Attractions

  • Jagannath Temple: A sacred Hindu temple and an important pilgrimage site.
  • Chilika Lake: India's largest coastal lagoon, home to migratory birds and dolphins.
  • Konark Sun Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its architectural grandeur.
  • Simlipal National Park: A biodiversity hotspot with tigers and waterfalls.

Interesting Facts

  • Odisha is the birthplace of **India's first Prime Minister of tribal origin**, Giridhar Gamang.
  • It has **one of the longest coastlines in India**, spanning around 485 km.
  • The **Odissi dance form** originated in Odisha and is over 2,000 years old.
  • The state has **the world's largest open-air theatre, Dhanu Yatra**.
