Manipur

Loktak Lake – The only floating lake in the world

Quick Facts

  • Area: 22,327 Sq. Kms
  • Capital: Imphal
  • Language: Meitei (Manipuri), English
  • Population: 2,855,794
  • Famous for: Loktak Lake, Kangla Fort, Keibul Lamjao National Park, Ima Keithel (Women's Market), Sangai Festival
  • Major Cities: Imphal, Thoubal, Bishnupur

Geographical & Economic Importance

Manipur is a land of **rolling hills and lush valleys**, with an economy primarily based on **agriculture, handloom, and handicrafts**. The state plays a crucial role in **India’s trade with Southeast Asia** due to its location near the Myanmar border.

It is also known for its production of **black rice (Chak-hao)**, which has gained global recognition for its high nutritional value.

Historical Significance

Manipur has a **rich historical heritage**, with its roots tracing back to the **Meitei Kingdom**. The **Battle of Imphal (1944)** during World War II was a significant turning point, where the British and Indian forces stopped the Japanese invasion.

The **Kangla Fort**, a symbol of Manipur's royal past, is an important historical site showcasing the legacy of its rulers.

Cultural Significance

Manipur is the birthplace of the **Ras Lila**, a classical dance form depicting the divine love of **Lord Krishna and Radha**.

  • Yaoshang Festival: The Manipuri version of Holi, celebrated with cultural performances and sports.
  • Ima Keithel: The world’s **largest women-run market**, showcasing Manipur’s unique social structure.
  • Sangai Festival: A grand cultural and tourism festival named after the rare **Sangai deer**, found only in Manipur.
  • Traditional Attire: The famous **Phanek and Innaphi** are beautifully woven garments worn by Manipuri women.

Tourist Attractions

  • Loktak Lake: The **largest freshwater lake in Northeast India**, famous for its **floating phumdis (islands)**.
  • Kangla Fort: An ancient fort that was the royal seat of the Manipuri kings.
  • Keibul Lamjao National Park: The world’s **only floating national park**, home to the endangered **Sangai deer**.
  • Shirui Hills: Famous for the **Shirui Lily**, a rare flower found only in this region.
  • Ukhrul: A picturesque hill town with stunning landscapes and cultural heritage.

Interesting Facts

  • Manipur is known as the **"Jewel of India"** due to its **scenic beauty and rich culture**.
  • The **Manipuri martial art "Thang-Ta"** is a traditional war technique involving swords and spears.
  • Manipur was an independent kingdom until it merged with India in **1949**.
  • The state has a **strong sports culture**, producing renowned athletes like **Mary Kom (Boxing Champion)**.

Traditional Cuisine

  • Eromba: A mashed vegetable dish blended with **fermented fish (Ngari)**.
  • Chak-hao Kheer: A delicious **black rice pudding**, known for its rich flavor and nutritional value.
  • Singju: A unique spicy Manipuri salad made with raw vegetables and local herbs.
  • Kangsoi: A healthy **stew made with seasonal vegetables** and local spices.
