Manipur

Loktak Lake – The Largest Freshwater Lake in Northeast India

Quick Facts

  • Area: 22,327 Sq. Kms
  • Capital: Imphal
  • Language: Meitei (Manipuri), English
  • Population: 2,855,794
  • Famous for: Loktak Lake, Kangla Fort, Keibul Lamjao National Park, Ima Keithel (Women's Market), Sangai Festival
  • Major Cities: Imphal, Thoubal, Bishnupur

Geographical & Ecological Significance

Manipur is located in **Northeast India**, sharing an international border with **Myanmar**. It is known for its **lush green landscapes, waterfalls, and biodiversity**.

Loktak Lake is unique because of its **phumdis** (floating biomass), and Keibul Lamjao National Park is the **only floating national park in the world**, home to the endangered **Sangai deer**.

Historical & Cultural Heritage

Manipur has a **rich cultural and martial history**, with the **Kangla Fort** in Imphal being a symbol of its past kingdom. It played a significant role in **World War II**, with battles fought in Imphal.

Dance forms like **Ras Lila**, associated with **Lord Krishna**, originated in Manipur. The state is also famous for its **indigenous martial art, Thang-Ta**.

Tourist Attractions

  • Loktak Lake: A floating lake with phumdis and a crucial water resource.
  • Kangla Fort: Historic palace of the Meitei rulers in Imphal.
  • Keibul Lamjao National Park: Home to the rare Sangai deer.
  • Ima Keithel: A unique **all-women market** run by thousands of female traders.
  • Sangai Festival: A celebration of Manipuri culture, traditions, and wildlife.

Economy & Handicrafts

Manipur has a thriving **handloom and handicrafts** industry. It is famous for:

  • Manipuri Handlooms: Unique textiles with intricate patterns.
  • Black Pottery (Longpi): Made from natural clay without a potter’s wheel.
  • Bamboo & Cane Products: Used for baskets, furniture, and home decor.

Interesting Facts

  • Manipur is known as the **"Jewel of India"** because of its natural beauty.
  • It is the birthplace of **Polo**, a sport that originated from the Manipuri game **Sagol Kangjei**.
  • Manipur is home to **rare orchids** and has over **500 species of medicinal plants**.
  • The **Nupi Lan (Women's War)** of 1904 and 1939 was a historical movement led by Manipuri women against British rule.

Delicacies & Cuisine

Manipuri cuisine is **organic, mildly spiced, and rice-based**.

  • Eromba: A spicy mashed dish made from fermented fish and vegetables.
  • Chamthong (Kangshoi): A vegetable stew served with rice.
  • Ngari: Fermented fish, a staple ingredient in Manipuri cooking.
  • Black Rice Kheer: A sweet dish made from **Chak-hao** (black rice), a GI-tagged Manipuri specialty.
