General Information

  • Area: 22,327 Sq. Kms
  • Capital: Imphal
  • Language: Meitei (Manipuri), English
  • Population: 2,855,794
  • Major Cities: Imphal, Thoubal, Bishnupur

Importance of Manipur

Manipur, known as the **"Jewel of India"**, is a state of breathtaking beauty, rich traditions, and historical significance. It plays a crucial role in India's **northeastern culture, biodiversity, and trade relations with Southeast Asia**.

Cultural Significance

Manipur is the birthplace of the **Ras Lila**, a classical dance form associated with Lord Krishna. The state celebrates numerous festivals, including **Yaoshang (Holi of Manipur), Lai Haraoba, and the Sangai Festival**, showcasing its vibrant heritage.

The state is also famous for **Ima Keithel**, the world's largest all-women market, symbolizing the **empowerment of women**.

Historical Significance

Manipur has a deep historical legacy, dating back to its powerful Meitei kingdom. It played a strategic role in **World War II**, with battles like the **Battle of Imphal (1944)** being a crucial turning point in the war.

Tourist Attractions

Some of Manipur's most popular destinations include:

  • Loktak Lake: The largest freshwater lake in Northeast India, famous for its **phumdis (floating islands)**.
  • Kangla Fort: A symbol of Manipuri history and a major archaeological site.
  • Keibul Lamjao National Park: The world's only **floating national park**, home to the endangered Sangai deer.

Economic Importance

Manipur's economy is **agriculture-driven**, with a focus on **handloom and handicrafts**. The state is known for its **black rice (Chak-hao)** and traditional bamboo products, which are highly valued across India.

Unique Cuisine

Manipur's cuisine is rich in flavors, featuring dishes like:

  • Eromba: A mashed vegetable dish with fermented fish.
  • Chak-hao Kheer: A delicious kheer made from black rice.
  • Ngari-based Curries: Fermented fish used in various traditional recipes.
