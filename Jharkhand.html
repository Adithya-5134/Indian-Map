Quick Facts

  • Capital: Ranchi
  • Languages: Hindi, Santali, Mundari, Ho
  • Population: 32,988,134
  • Famous for: Betla National Park, Hundru Falls, Dassam Falls, Parasnath Hill, Tribal Culture
  • Major Cities: Ranchi, Jamshedpur, Dhanbad, Bokaro

Geographical & Economic Importance

Jharkhand is known as the "Mineral State of India," contributing to over 40% of India’s mineral resources. The state is rich in coal, iron ore, copper, mica, and uranium, making it a key industrial hub.

The steel city of Jamshedpur is home to Tata Steel, one of the largest steel manufacturers in India. Jharkhand’s forests cover over 29% of its total area, supporting a thriving timber and lac industry.
