Haryana

Sultanpur National Park – A Haven for Birdwatchers

Quick Facts

  • Area: 44,212 Sq. Kms
  • Capital: Chandigarh
  • Language: Hindi, Haryanvi
  • Population: 2,53,53,081
  • Famous for: Surajkund Mela, Sultanpur National Park, Kingdom of Dreams, Kurukshetra, Sheetla Mata Mandir
  • Major Cities: Gurgaon, Faridabad, Rohtak, Panipat

Importance of Haryana

Haryana is one of India’s **most industrialized states**, with major hubs like **Gurgaon and Faridabad** playing a key role in India’s economic growth. It is home to **Maruti Suzuki’s manufacturing units** and **several IT and automobile industries**.

The state also plays a crucial role in India’s **agriculture sector**, contributing significantly to the country's **wheat and rice production**.

Historical Significance

Haryana is known for **Kurukshetra**, the legendary battlefield of the **Mahabharata**, where Lord Krishna delivered the **Bhagavad Gita** to Arjuna.

Panipat, another historically significant city, witnessed three major **battles that shaped India's history**—the **Battles of Panipat (1526, 1556, and 1761)**.

Cultural Heritage

  • Folk Dances: Haryana is famous for its **Ghoomar, Jhumar, and Phag Dance**.
  • Surajkund Mela: One of India’s largest **handicraft fairs**, attracting artisans from across the country.
  • Festivals: Haryana celebrates **Teej, Baisakhi, and Diwali** with grandeur.
  • Haryanvi Cuisine: Includes **Bajra Khichdi, Singri ki Sabzi, Kachri ki Chutney**, and **Lassi**.

Tourist Attractions

  • Surajkund Mela: A world-famous **craft fair** showcasing traditional arts.
  • Kingdom of Dreams: India’s first **live entertainment, theatre, and leisure** destination.
  • Brahma Sarovar (Kurukshetra): A sacred water tank mentioned in **Hindu scriptures**.
  • Sultanpur National Park: A major **bird sanctuary**, home to over **250 species of migratory birds**.
  • Sheetla Mata Mandir (Gurgaon): A popular **pilgrimage site** dedicated to Goddess Sheetla.

Interesting Facts

  • Kurukshetra is known as the **"Land of the Bhagavad Gita"**.
  • Haryana has the **highest number of rural crorepatis** in India.
  • It is the **largest exporter of Basmati rice** in India.
  • Gurgaon is a major **IT and business hub**, often called **"Millennium City"**.
