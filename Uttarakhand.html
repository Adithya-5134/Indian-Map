Quick Facts

  • Area: 53,483 Sq. Kms
  • Capital: Dehradun
  • Language: Hindi, Garhwali, Kumaoni
  • Population: 1,01,16,752
  • Famous for: Char Dham Yatra, Kedarnath, Badrinath, Nainital, Valley of Flowers, Rishikesh, Haridwar Ganga Aarti
  • Major Cities: Dehradun, Haridwar, Rishikesh, Nainital

Geographical and Economic Importance

Uttarakhand is a **Himalayan state** known for its **mountainous terrain, dense forests, and river valleys**. The state is home to the **Ganges and Yamuna rivers**, making it a vital water source. Tourism, hydropower, and agriculture play a crucial role in its economy.

Historical Significance

Uttarakhand has been a **center of spirituality and learning** since ancient times. It is home to the **Char Dham pilgrimage sites** â€“ Kedarnath, Badrinath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri. The region finds mention in **Hindu epics like the Mahabharata and Ramayana**.

Cultural Significance

Uttarakhand has a **rich cultural heritage** influenced by **Garhwali and Kumaoni traditions**. Festivals like **Makar Sankranti, Nanda Devi Mela, and Ganga Dussehra** are celebrated with grandeur. The state is also famous for its **folk music and traditional Pahari dance forms**.

Tourist Attractions

  • Har Ki Pauri (Haridwar): A sacred ghat on the Ganges, famous for the evening **Ganga Aarti**.
  • Nainital Lake: A beautiful **hill station** known for its scenic lake and pleasant weather.
  • Valley of Flowers: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with **rare Himalayan flora and breathtaking landscapes**.
  • Rishikesh: The **Yoga Capital of the World**, famous for **spiritual retreats and adventure sports**.

Interesting Facts

  • Uttarakhand is known as **"Devbhoomi" (Land of Gods)** due to its numerous temples and pilgrimage sites.
  • The **Jim Corbett National Park** is India's oldest national park and a major **tiger conservation site**.
  • Rishikesh hosts the **International Yoga Festival**, attracting yoga enthusiasts from around the world.
  • Uttarakhand has India's **second-highest peak, Nanda Devi** (7,816 meters).
