Bihar

Mahabodhi Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site

  • Area: 94,163 Sq. Kms
  • Capital: Patna
  • Language: Hindi, Maithili, Bhojpuri
  • Population: 10,40,99,452
  • Famous for: Mahabodhi Temple, Nalanda University, Vikramshila, Madhubani Paintings, Chhath Puja, Litti Chokha
  • Major Cities: Patna, Gaya, Bhagalpur, Muzaffarpur

Significance of Bihar

Bihar is considered the **cradle of Indian civilization**. It was home to two major empires, the **Maurya Empire** (led by Emperor Ashoka) and the **Gupta Empire**, which contributed to India's Golden Age. Bihar is also where **Buddhism** originated, as Lord Buddha attained enlightenment in **Bodh Gaya**.

Historical Importance

Bihar housed two of the world's **most prestigious universities**—**Nalanda University** and **Vikramshila University**, which attracted scholars from China, Korea, and Central Asia. The ruins of these universities are a testament to Bihar’s rich educational history.
