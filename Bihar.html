Bihar

Mahabodhi Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site

Quick Facts

  • Area: 94,163 Sq. Kms
  • Capital: Patna
  • Language: Hindi, Maithili, Bhojpuri
  • Population: 10,40,99,452
  • Famous for: Mahabodhi Temple, Nalanda University, Vikramshila, Madhubani Paintings, Chhath Puja, Litti Chokha
  • Major Cities: Patna, Gaya, Bhagalpur, Muzaffarpur

Importance of Bihar

Bihar is considered as the cradle of Indian civilization.

It was home to two major empires, the Maurya Empire (led by Emperor Ashoka) and the Gupta Empire, which contributed to India's Golden Age.

Bihar is also where Buddhism originated, as Lord Buddha attained enlightenment in Bodh Gaya.

The state has played a pivotal role in shaping India's history, culture, and economy.

The Bihar plains, enriched by the Ganges River, make the region highly fertile, supporting agriculture and trade.

Additionally, Bihar is known for its art and literature, with its unique Madhubani paintings gaining global recognition.

Historical Significance

Bihar housed two of the world's most prestigious universities—Nalanda University and Vikramshila University, which attracted scholars from China, Korea, and Central Asia. These universities were centers of learning and intellectual exchange, making Bihar a significant seat of education in ancient India.

The state has witnessed several historic battles and movements, including India’s first freedom movement in 1857 and the Champaran Satyagraha led by Mahatma Gandhi.

Bihar was also a key location during the Mauryan and Gupta dynasties, with Patna (ancient Pataliputra) serving as an important capital city.

Tourist Attractions

  • Mahabodhi Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage site where Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment.
  • Nalanda University: The ancient seat of learning, known for its vast library and global reputation.
  • Vikramshila University: Another prestigious learning center, showcasing Bihar’s educational legacy.
  • Bodh Gaya: A sacred Buddhist pilgrimage site featuring the Bodhi Tree and monasteries.
  • Rajgir: A historically significant town associated with Lord Buddha and Mahavira.
  • Valmiki National Park: A wildlife sanctuary known for its rich biodiversity and tiger reserve.

Interesting Facts

  • Bihar is the birthplace of two major religions — Buddhism and Jainism.
  • The ancient Nalanda University is recognized as one of the world’s first residential universities.
  • Bihar is famous for its Chhath Puja, one of the biggest festivals dedicated to the Sun God.
  • The state has the largest youth population in India, making it a hub for education and talent.
  • Bihar is home to Litti Chokha, a traditional dish that is popular across India.
