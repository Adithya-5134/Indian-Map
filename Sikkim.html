Sikkim

Kanchenjunga - The Third Highest Mountain in the World

Quick Facts

  • Area: 7,096 Sq. Kms
  • Capital: Gangtok
  • Language: Nepali, Bhutia, Lepcha, Hindi, English
  • Population: 6,10,577
  • Famous for: Kanchenjunga, Nathula Pass, Yumthang Valley, Rumtek Monastery, Organic Farming
  • Major Cities: Gangtok, Namchi, Geyzing, Mangan

Geographical and Economic Importance

Sikkim is a small but significant state in Northeast India, sharing borders with Nepal, Bhutan, and Tibet. It is the least populous and second smallest state in India. The economy is mainly based on tourism and organic farming, making it India's first fully organic state.

Historical Significance

Sikkim was an independent kingdom until 1975 when it became the 22nd state of India. It has a rich history influenced by Buddhist culture, with important monasteries like Rumtek Monastery.

Cultural Significance

Sikkim has a diverse culture with influences from Tibetan Buddhism. Festivals like Losar, Saga Dawa, and Pang Lhabsol are celebrated with great enthusiasm. The state is also known for its traditional dances like the Mask Dance.

Tourist Attractions

  • Gangtok: The vibrant capital city with MG Marg and Buddhist monasteries.
  • Nathula Pass: A high-altitude mountain pass connecting India and China.
  • Yumthang Valley: Known as the 'Valley of Flowers' of the East.
  • Rumtek Monastery: One of the most important Tibetan Buddhist monasteries.

Interesting Facts

  • Sikkim is the first 100% organic state in India.
  • It has the highest per capita income in Northeast India.
  • It is home to India's highest mountain, Kanchenjunga.
  • Sikkim has a 100% literate workforce with high human development.
